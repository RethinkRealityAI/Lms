### S9 — Expandable List: Overview of Emerging Therapies
**Type:** `expandable-list`
**LMS block:** `content_list` (with expandable/accordion items)

**Title:** Overview of Emerging Therapies

Items (title → content):

**L-Glutamine:**
"Mechanism:• Reduces oxidative stress by synthesizing NADPH and glutathioneEvidence:• 2 RCTs - Phase II and Phase III, placebo controlled• Phase III: Lower cumulative pain crises at 48 weeks"

**Canakinumab:**
"Mechanism:• Selective blockade of IL-1b to reduce inflammationEvidence:• Phase II placebo controlled randomized control trial of 49 participants• Reduced hemolysis, number and duration of hospitalizations"

**Crovalimab:**
"Mechanism:• Complement blockade (C5) which reduces ongoing inflammation in the setting of VOC/endothelial activation/hemolysisEvidence:• Phase Ib Crosswalk-A trial in progress• Subcutaneous injection"

**Voxelotor:**
"Mechanism:• Hemoglobin S polymerization inhibitor, increased oxygen affinity resulting in increased red cell survival and reduced hemolysisEvidence:• HOPE trial (Phase III)• Hb increased by at least 10 points in the high dose (89%) vs placebo (25%) at 72 weeks"

**Decitabine and Tetrahydrouridine:**
"Mechanism:• DNMT1 is the enzyme that silences HbF from infancy onwards• Decitabine interferes with the function of DNMT1• Tetrahydrouridine inhibits enzyme that rapidly deactivates DecitabineEvidence:• Phase 1 trial• Increased Hb 12-19 g/L• HbF increased by 4-9%"

**GBT021601:**
"Mechanism:• GBT021601 stabilizes hemoglobin (Hb) in the oxygenated state to inhibit HbS polymerization• Longer half-life than voxelotor and potential to achieve higher Hb occupanciesEvidence:• Phase 2 Part A dose-finding study• Mean increase in Hb from baseline was 2.67g/dL for the 100-mg group and 3.17g/dL for the 150-mg group"

**Mitapivat:**
"Mechanism:• Activator of pyruvate kinase (involved in RBC metabolism)• Increases adenosine triphosphate (ATP) which improves RBC survival• Decreases 2,3 diphosphoglycerate (2, 3-DPG) which increases oxygen affinity of HbEvidence:• Phase 2 double-blind trial• Hb response in 46.2% of 50mg arm and 50% of 100mg arm"

**dWIZ-2:**
"Mechanism:• Targeted Degradation of the Wiz Transcription Factor for Gamma Globin De-Repression• Cereblon (CRBN) is a naturally existing protein degradation machinery in cells• dWIZ-2 degrades WIZ and induces HbF in CD34+ derived erythrobalstsEvidence:• CD34+ derived erythroblasts from sickle cell disease donors and mouse xenotransplantation model"

**AB1:**
"Mechanism:• AB1 is a potent non-cytotoxic agent which depletes DNMT1 to induce HbF in sickle erythroid progenitors (Li et al., unpublished data)Evidence:• In SCD Townes mice model, AB1 increases γ-globin mRNA and F-cells, decreases sickle cell phenotype in blood, improves splenic hematopoiesis"

### S12 — Curative vs Non-Curative
**Type:** `scrolling-media`
**LMS block:** `rich_text` + `image_gallery`

**Title:** Curative vs Non-Curative Therapies

Content items:
- "Let's split the options into curative and non-curative therapies. We will cover curative therapies in detail in Lesson 3."
- [IMAGE]
- "Photo credit: Salinas Cisneros G & Thein SL (2020)"
- "Starting with non-curative options, recall the pathobiology of SCD: With the Hb S mutation, the hemoglobin becomes deoxygenated. There is increased hydrophobicity and the hemoglobin polymerizes (becomes sticky), causing the red blood cells to become sickled. Options to interfere with the sickle process include: a) improving oxygen affinity of Hb b) Increasing Fetal Hemoglobin (HbF)"
- [IMAGE]
- "HbS Polymerization can be affected by: 1. Hemoglobin oxygen affinity, 2. Hemoglobin concentration, 3. Delay time, 4. Other (hyperviscosity, temperature)"
- "Increasing HbF is the target of gene therapies because HbF does not polymerize and can actually prevent polymerization of HbS. In general, >20-30% HbF levels are required to prevent sickling."
